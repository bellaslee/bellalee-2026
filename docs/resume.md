# Interactive Resume Page Plan

## Purpose
Create an explorable resume that communicates:
- professional experience
- technical thinking
- impact and growth
- depth of capability

This page should feel structured, credible, and calm — more like a well-designed product dashboard than a PDF.

---

## Page Layout

### Desktop Layout
Two-column structure.

**Left Sidebar (sticky)**
- Overview
- Experience
- Case Studies
- Skills
- Writing / Notes
- Education & Certifications

**Right Main Panel**
Dynamic content based on section selection.

---

## Section 1 — Overview

### Header Block
- Name
- Role title (e.g. Software Engineer / Consultant)
- One-sentence professional positioning statement

### Snapshot Grid
Small cards showing:
- Years of experience
- Core domains (e.g. systems, integrations, tooling)
- Current learning focus
- Location / work style (optional)

### Key Strengths
3–5 concise bullets:
- system design thinking  
- stakeholder communication  
- ambiguity navigation  
- rapid onboarding  

---

## Section 2 — Experience

Each role should be displayed as an expandable card.

### Role Card (Collapsed State)
- Company (or anonymized descriptor)
- Role title
- Dates
- One-line summary of scope

### Role Card (Expanded State)

**Context**
- industry / environment
- scale of organization
- team structure

**Responsibilities**
- key ownership areas
- collaboration scope

**Impact Highlights**
- outcomes framed in qualitative terms  
- process or capability improvements  
- technical leadership signals  

**Capabilities Demonstrated**
Tag-style labels such as:
- integration architecture  
- async workflows  
- enterprise constraints  
- cross-functional alignment  

**Related Case Study Link**
Deep link to:
`/projects/project-slug`

---

## Section 3 — Case Studies

Grid or stacked layout.

Each entry includes:
- Project title
- Short problem statement
- Constraint summary
- Your role in approach/design
- “View deep dive” link

Avoid sensitive details. Focus on:
- reasoning
- tradeoffs
- learning outcomes

---

## Section 4 — Skills

### Skill Clusters (not long keyword lists)

**Industries**
- legal
- financial services

**Technical**
- API design  
- debugging complex systems  
- onboarding into legacy codebases  

**Collaboration & Leadership**
- technical storytelling  
- aligning stakeholders  
- mentoring  

---

## Section 5 — Writing / Notes

Short editorial section linking externally to digital garden.

Include:
- brief description of writing focus  
- 2–3 featured essays  
- external link with icon  

Purpose:
Show reflective thinking and communication ability.

---

## Section 6 — Education & Certifications

Clean, compact layout.

Include:
- degree(s)
- relevant coursework (optional)
- certifications in progress or completed
- structured learning milestones tied to roadmap

---

## Interaction & UX Details

- Sidebar remains visible while scrolling.
- Expanding a role smoothly pushes content downward.
- Skill tags filter visible experience entries.
- Case study links open dedicated deep-dive pages.
- External writing links open in a new tab.
- Maintain generous whitespace and restrained borders.
